About RenderMan University

Many moons ago, in a distant web archive, RenderMan University was created.

Hearing and understanding the needs of 3D artists of all levels, the RenderMan team is pleased to introduce you to the revamped RenderMan University, providing a central campus for all your RenderMan educational needs.

Whether you’re new to RenderMan or an industry veteran, RenderMan University is packed full of training material to help new 3D artists get up to speed on RenderMan, through to the latest rendering developments and advanced topics.

An ever-evolving learning resource, RenderMan University will continue to grow in content, working closely with our fellow Pixarians and friends at Pixar University.

Pixar Tutors

Who’s involved?

Eugene Riecansky
After 30 years of creating films, motion graphics, and CG for brands such as Red Bull, Jim Carrey, Massive Attack, The Prodigy & Madonna, Eugene joined Pixar and has turned his focus to inspiring and mentoring like-minded creatives by helping them achieve their creative goals & ambitions.

Eugene brings all his years of experience forward to you in the form of training, example scenes, and mentoring.

Dylan Sisson

Dylan Sisson is an accomplished digital artist with over two decades of experience in the field of visual effects and animation.

In 1999, Dylan joined Pixar Animation Studios and has since been instrumental in the development and promotion of Pixar’s core rendering technology, RenderMan. As the designer and creator of the RenderMan Walking Teapot, Dylan has established a reputation as a creative innovator in the industry.

Aside from his work at Pixar, Dylan is dedicated to exploring the potential of new technologies and inspiring others to do the same.

Leif Pedersen

Leif Pedersen is a CG Generalist with over a decade of experience, applying his diverse skills to enhance RenderMan and ensure it meets the evolving needs of artists and studios worldwide.

Additionally, Leif actively engages with the RenderMan Community and enjoys showcasing the most artistic and technical aspects of the renderer.
