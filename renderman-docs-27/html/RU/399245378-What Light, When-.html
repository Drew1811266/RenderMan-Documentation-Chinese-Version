RenderMan ships with a full set of analytic and photorealistic lights.

This quick guide below aims to help you determine which light to use for what lighting purpose.

RenderMan Light

Uses

PxrDomeLight

In much visual effects and animation work, scenes are lit primarily using a High Dynamic Range Image (HDRI) or a hand-painted sky ramp texture.

They can also be used to add a rim light to a character, as they provide a nice, even rim-lighting effect.

PxrRectLight

A large rectangular light that mimics a photographic light panel or softbox.

One of the most commonly used lights in rendering.

For realistic reflections and light quality, apply a texture to the light.

A general light used in lighting for many looks and can also be used for adding interior fill light from a ceiling or wall.

This is also used to add bounce lighting from walls or bounce board geometry.

PxrDiscLight

Same as a PxrRectLight, just in a disc shape

PxrDistantLight

Mainly use this for mimicking sunlight, moonlight, or a very distant light source

PxrSphereLight

Often used to replicate practical light sources that emit light 360 degrees, such as candles, headlights, and lightbulbs.

PxrCylinderLight

Great for interior tube lighting, neons, and light sabers!

PxrPortalLight

Windows!

PxrEnvDaylight

Daylight simulations are helpful when you want a quick, easy setup for exterior lighting or maybe some look development time, but you don't have an HDRI for the PxrDomeLight. The simplicity and realism make this light a favorite for artists.
